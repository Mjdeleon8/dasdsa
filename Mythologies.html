Bathala

In Tagalog mythology, Bathala is the supreme deity and creator of all things. He is often depicted as a god of the sky and is associated with the sun.

Maria Makiling

Maria Makiling is a well-known folklore character, often depicted as a beautiful fairy who resides in Mount Makiling. Sheis said to protect the mountain and its forests.

Tikbalang

The Tikbalang is a creature with the body of a horse and the head of a man. It is known to mislead travelers in the forest and play tricks on them.

Manananggal

The Manananggal is a vampire-like creature that can separate its upper body from its lower half and fly at night to feed on the blood of pregnant women.

Aswang

Aswang is a generic term for various shape-shifting creatures in Filipino folklore, often associated with evil and theconsumption of human flesh.

Dalaketnon

In Visayan mythology, the Dalaketnon are mythical bird-human creatures that are said to inhabit the forests. They are often associated with protecting the environment.

Bakunawa

Bakunawa is a giant sea serpent in Visayan mythology. It is believed to be responsible for lunar eclipses and is often depicted as a fearsome dragon.

Kapre

The Kapre is a tree-dwelling creature, often described as a giant with red eyes, that smokes a huge tobacco pipe. It is known to play pranks on humans.

Mangindusa

In Bicolano mythology, the Mangindusa is a goddess of punishment and retribution. She is believed to bring misfortune to those who have committed wrongdoings.
