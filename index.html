Traditions in the Philippines that slowly forgotten

The Filipino culture is a rich fusion of traditional Filipino customs and Spanish Catholic influences, infused with elements from America and other Asian regions. Known for their strong emphasis on family ties and religious values, Filipinos also have a deep appreciation for art, fashion, music, and culinary delights.

1. Traditional Games

Photo credit: Dickie Aguado

Those who have engaged in traditional Filipino games like piko, patintero, or tumbang-preso are fortunate to have experienced the joy these games bring. Unfortunately, contemporary children tend to favor digital devices such as iPads, Xbox, and mobile games over the outdoor play and camaraderie with fellow kids.

2. Filipino Folk Beliefs

Photo credit: https://www.filwebasia.com/10-funniest-filipino-superstitions/

Folk beliefs, also referred to as "superstitious beliefs," are integral to a community's values and culture. They mirror the customs, traditions, and norms of a group, often rooted in religious beliefs, opinions, or age-old practices. These beliefs offer insights into how a community perceives the unknown and the rituals to appease the gods shaping their destiny. However, in today's era, many millennials dismiss these superstitious beliefs as mere sayings held by the older generation, considering them unreal and not applicable to their lives.

3. Traditional Clothing

Photo credit: https://sepiaera.wordpress.com/2013/06/05/women-in-traditional-dress-early-1900s/

In the past, Filipinos adhered to conservative attire, avoiding the display of too much skin in public, particularly among girls. However, significant shifts have occurred in Filipino fashion, largely influenced by Western countries.

4. Pagmamano

Photo credit: filipiknow.net

The "Mano" or "pagmamano" is a traditional Filipino gesture signifying respect for elders and seeking their blessings. In this gesture, reminiscent of hand-kissing, the person bowing towards the elder gently presses their forehead to the elder's hand. Regrettably, this meaningful tradition is fading away, gradually replaced by alternative forms of showing respect, such as the "beso-beso" (cheek-to-cheek greeting). While still respectful, it lacks the distinctive charm of the original pagmamano.

5. Traditional Courtships

Photo credit: Crispin V. Lopez

The Harana, a serenading tradition in rural Philippines, served as a formal way for young men to meet single ladies. However, with the advent of technology, the landscape of dating has transformed. Today, teenagers often utilize platforms like Messenger or DMs to express their "panliligaw" to someone they fancy. A simple message, a hopeful reply, and the rest becomes history. In many parts of the country, traditional courtship practices, like harana and paninilbihan (where a suitor performs chores for the girl's family to prove his love), are on the brink of extinction due to these changes.
